When I was in school, there was a well just outside the school property beside the playground. 
It was said that if you stare into it for long enough it will stare back at you. Many of the students 
had tried staring into the well during breaks or lunch time but without anything happening out of the
ordinary. Well there was one time when a student, who left his bag on the playground, went to get his
bag. When he didn't came back after 10 minutes or so, the teacher sent another student to check. He 
found that the student had fallen into the well. He quickly called the teacher and the student was  
safely pulled out. The student later said that he didn't remember going anywhere near the well. 
After that incident student were prohibited from going near the well. The rule was not strictly enforced.
After a couple of months it was like the rule didn't even existed. 

The students of grade 9 would be responsible for managing the sports equipment. It was a tedious task for
whoever was in charge but it also meant that you could play whenever you wanted. The day before the summer
vacation began, our class decided to have a football match with the other section. Although I rarely played
football, the game between our two section was the most exciting which is why I agreed to see it through and
then return all the equipment after the game to the store even though I knew it would be late evening when 
the game ended. I was right and the game lasted until 8 o'clock. My classmates helped me bring the footballs
and other stuff to the store room. I was about to lock the store room and head home when I realized on of the
football was missing. I hurriedly returned to the ground and started looking for the ball. I was about to give
up and look for it the next day. It was already really late and my parent would have been worried sick that I
wasn't home.  When I turned back towards the gate,  I heard the ball roll and hit something. I turned towards
the direction from where the sound came. The ball was at the foot of the well. I ran towards the well. I 
slowly bent over, lowering my hands towards the ball. The clouds shadowed over the moon so it was hard to
see the old ball. I felt the rough surface of the ball and as I was about to pick it up, I looked into the
well. There was nothing at first, just the calm stillness of the water, then two ripples right next to each
other, then small pair of eyes at the center of ripples, then i heard a splash and another one, then i felt
cold, then there was nothing. So if you by any chance come near this well, stare into it and I might just 
stare back at you.
