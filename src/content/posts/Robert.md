Robert was tense lately. The crimes and investigations, affirming the victim's family, catching the criminal,
being a detective meant he was always tense but for last few days he has been especially uneasy. The end of 
the year left him with uneasiness and he has been strained since. Robert was the only detective in this 
little town after the disappearance of his partner on halloween last year. That case was still open along
with a few minor ones of theft and robbery. So when the mayor's kid disappeared on  the new years eve his
workload multiplied by a factor of two and his uneasiness by a factor of ten.

Ryan's parents had separated when he was 12. So for last two years Ryan celebrated some holidays at his mothers
while some at his fathers. Although he lived mostly with his father, he had a habit of going to his mother's house
on a whim without telling his dad. On new year he was supposed to be at his friend's party and had told his father
he would be late. His father had plans of his own to meet with the police department so he agreed. The mayor was
held back at the party. By the time he was home, he was tired and a little tipsy from the alcohol so when he 
noticed his son was not home, he thought  he went to his mothers and went to sleep. Mayor cooper marriage had
not ended on the best of terms. He had found out his wife had been cheating on him. They had a huge argument then
after few weeks got divorced. Jon had decided not to tell his son the real reason why his parents were separating.
Ryan was more close to his mother than him so he thought it was for the best. He was very happy when he got the
custody and Ryan, too, was fine with it. He was slowly but surely getting closer to his son and Ryan was beginning
to open up to him more. So whenever Ryan went to his mother's, Jon let him do it without any objection. When Ryan
hadn't returned the day after too he decided to phone his wife. "Why isn't he home yet? He has school today." Jon
said into the phone trying not to let his anger out. "What are you taking about?"  his wife's voice was cold and
snapped back like a whip. "Ryan! Is he not there?" "Ryan hasn't came here since Christmas". Jon ended the call and
quickly dialed Ethan. "Good morning may-". "Is Ryan there?". After hearing No, he called detective Robert and told
him everything. 

Robert was a young man in his mid twenties. Despite that he was the head detective in this town. He had his dad
to thank for all that.  Robert was not very close with his family. When he was a kid, his siblings though of him
as extremely weird and never played with him. Even when he was in college, He didn't show interest in anything.
So when he told his dad that he wanted to become a detective, he quickly pulled a few strings and got him the 
appointment as a head detective in this town and sent him away with so much money he wouldn't have to ask his 
dad for money again. 

Robert had an office for working on cases in his bedroom. But when he liked to be fully concentrated, he has a
special place in his basement. Ever since the disappearance of his partner and more so after Ryan's, he mostly
worked in his basement. His daily routine had changed even more since yesterday. He would wake up, drink a glass
of water, he didn't like tea or coffee, fill up his water bottle and go to the basement. He was on the verge of
cracking the case so he spent most of the time there. He would come back up at 10, quickly fire up some food and
take it with him again into the basement. Stay there until the evening then come back up, go to the station, see
if anything had come up and come back, cook dinner take it with him to the basement and then come back at 12 to 
sleep. Looking at the furniture, of which there were rarely any, you couldn't guess, Robert had a bright 
personality and a very active person. The only furniture in his bed room was a clock, whose hour and minute hands
were at 8 and 12 and second had although moving barely made a noise, a sofa in the living room and a table and
a chair in the kitchen. The wallpaper was a bland green without any decorations. The only piece of noise made in
the house came from the stairs leading down to the basement and the door that revealed the stairs. The door 
opened slowly accompanied by the loud creaking sound. There were no light all the way up to the basement door.
Only faint light could be seen coming from inside the basement when Robert was working there. The basement was
identical to the one above and you could guess that it even made the same sound. The door suddenly opened. Robert
came out, closed the door and quickly ran up. Had he done it? Did he solve the case?
