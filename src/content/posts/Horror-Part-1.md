I don't know when or how it started but I've loved horror stories, movies, folklore and anything else related
to it for as long as I can remember. Paranormal stuff , to me, has always been fascinating. As a kid I loved 
listening to tales of paranormal encounters, spirits, entities that remained in the dark etc even if I felt
scared listening to it.

## The Skeptic
I don't believe in any of the paranormal events, entities and stories which I so love consuming. I find
that a little sad. I mean would't it be so much more interesting if the movies we watch, novels and short
stories we read, scary anecdotes we hear from out friends or relatives were actually true. Imagine if there
was actually a scary monster under you bed right now waiting for you to fall asleep so he could feed on your
life or whatever they do.  Personally I love anything related to darkness. Let me give you an example.

*Imagine you wake up from your sleep because you really have to pee. You notice that the power is out so
you try to find your phone but without any success. Now the uneasiness is slowly creeping in. You try to
pay no attention to any thought that comes to your mind and feeling the way using your hands and legs and
get up from bed. You reach the door and open it. The door opens making a sound similar to that of a sad 
old cat dying. Again you try to shake the feeling but this time you can't fully get rid of it. You think
for a moment if you should just wait till morning but the pee is about to come out. You start walking 
towards the toilet at the end of the hallway. You feel like you have been walking for quite a while but you
still haven't reached the toilet. Finally you reach it, open the door and quickly close it. You start to pee
and it keeps coming out and out. You are wondering how much more is left when your eyes stop at the corner 
of the bathroom. Your gaze seemed fixed at the corner as if your head has been held by something invisible 
not allowing it to move. The more you stare at it the more the darkness seem to stare back. Finally the 
pee stopped coming out. You move towards the wall to wash your hands. You avoid looking at the corner and
just focus on washing your hand. As you are washing your hands, you feel a warm air on your legs. You 
quickly close the water tap and come out of the bathroom and close the door behind you as soon as possible. 
You run back to your room, jump in your bed and hide under the quilt.*

Imagine the sleep after that. But alas that is never going to happen. Right?
