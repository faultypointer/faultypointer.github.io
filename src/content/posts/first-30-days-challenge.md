# What?
For the next 30 days (starting from 25 june 2024), I'm challenging myself to complete a piece of writing however small or large. It may be a short
story, a poem (with my ability to see future, I can tell that only 1 poem will be written for this challenge), or maybe even a rant. 

# Whyyyy?
## a little bit of history
I have always been fond of stories specially the scary ones. I tired to write some stories and did so and unsuprisingly they were without
heads or tails. Although I have lost those stories, I have not lost the interest in reading and writing them. That didn't mean I read much less 
write them. For the longest time I tired to read the stories on phone and failed without even completing the first chapter. I don't know why 
but reading them on phone was the least interesting thing to do. I can say that now as I have started reading hard copies and thats one 
of my favourite things to do. 

A couple of years ago I did a trial run to see if I really liked reading books and was only held back by my choice of medium or I didn't
like reading at all. You can guess the result of that. And so last year I brought a bunch of books and read them. I was surprised at how quickly I
finished some of them. I even finished a book completely reading it on laptop and am currently halfway through another one. Now that I confirmed
I liked reading, read a bunch of books and even finished reading a book digitally, writing should be the next step.

## but why now?
well its very simple. I try to learn japanese, go through the basics and few of the kanjis then something would come up, maybe an exam or won't
have access to my devices for some time or other reasons, the flow would break, then I will lack motivation to pick it up again for some time
the finally after many days I would try to learn japanese and repeat. This has been going on since I was in 12th grade.

**What has this got to do with writing?**

everything. I realized I'm never gonna be able to learn japanese like this so either I'd have to keep learning japanese everyday no matter
what (well thats not gonna happen anytime soon) or would have to give up. And so I did and decided to pick up writing as my hobby.

## okay but still don't get the challenge stuff
with my long history of abandoing the projects I start, I didn't want to "learn" how to write then write something and finally giving up. Actually
thats not true, I would've given up after a day or two of trying to "learn" how to write. So I decided I would just write it.
