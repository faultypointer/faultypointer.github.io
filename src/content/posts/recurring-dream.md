I have been having this weird recurring dream for the past few months. They don't have any fixed interval between them. The next one might occur the very next day or even take a month. I've only ever told very few of my friends about this. It's my first time sharing these weird dreams online. I don't remember the exact date but it all began six or seven months ago. It was around the time my best friend Ryan went missing.

I was running through the woods alone. It was dark but I could still see, although not clearly. I didn't know where I was, there were no forests or anything like that near where I lived, or why I was running. All I remembered was that I was scared. That struck me as odd. I mean I'm not easily scared. It seem rather weird taking about myself like that, but I'm the type of person to face any problem head on. I wanted to turn around and look at what I was running from but my mind kept saying "Don't turn around. No matter what you do, DO NOT TURN AROUND." I kept running but I was really curious. I mean I didn't know what I was running from, why was I even scared. I stopped. After the sound I was making while running had ceased, I could hear my heart beat. *thump-thump-thuMP-THUMP-THUMP* I turned around very slowly. I saw nothing. I heard nothing. My heart began to beat even faster. *THUMPTHUMPTHUMPTHUMPTHUMP*  That's when I heard a sound coming from my back. It sounded like a branch cracking. I quickly turned around to see a shadow move behind some trees ahead. My fears had vanished at that point but my heart still kept beating faster and faster. I ran towards the sound now keeping my eyes ahead. I could feel something in my hands, I was gripping it tightly but I didn't know what it was. I didn't even bother to look at it. I just keep running towards the trees. When I was finally about to see what was behind those trees, I woke up. 

I was all sweaty when I woke up the next day. I tried to forget the weird dream and went to school. Ryan noticed something was wrong and asked me if something happened. Ryan and I were best friends from childhood. We both would know right away if anything was on each other's mind. I told him about the dream. He tried to cheer me up and after some time we were both laughing about it. By the time I went home, I barely remembered about the weird dream. I went to sleep that night feeling just as better as I was the night before. I was running through the woods again. It seemed like it was the same place but something felt different. I was scared again and my heart was beating faster. But this time I knew why I was scared. I knew something was chasing me and that I should keep running. I didn't want to look back. I kept running and running and running. My legs were growing weaker and I could hear my breadths getting louder. My vision was getting blurry. That's when I tripped and fell. I knew that the thing that was chasing me heard that. It was coming for me quicker and quicker. I tried to get up and run but I was too tired. My legs gave out. I sat beside some trees. I closed my eyes and prayed that he wouldn't find me. I was praying when I felt something in front of me. When I opened my eyes, I saw the sharp ax, shinning in the moonlight, coming towards me.

I woke up again sweats all over me. This time I wasn't worried like yesterday, maybe second time it had lost its uneasiness. I went to school that day early. I was waiting for Ryan to tell him about the dream but he didn't show up. After the school was over, I went over to Ryan's. His house was locked and his parents were nowhere to be seen. I thought at the time that maybe he and his parents went over to his grandma's house. He was telling me that his grandma was sick. I went home after that. That night I didn't have that weird dream and if I remember correctly I didn't have that dream for quite some time. 

It had been few weeks since Ryan had disappeared. Some of the students in the class began to make theories. Some said he ran away, while some said he was kidnapped. I was so sick of hearing them talk about Ryan that I left the classroom and went to the roof. It seemed Sam saw me leaving the classroom making a face that looked like I was about to knock someone out. He came to the roof and sat beside me. "I heard what those guys were taking about Ryan. I can't imagine how you must feel." Sam was a year or two younger that me and the rest of the class for that matter. Despite that he was the most considerate and matured. "Me feeling angry at them isn't going to bring Ryan back. I am more frustrated at the fact that no one seems to know what happened to him. The police are doing all they can but there haven't been anything about him. He seemed fine and lively the day before." I told Sam everything about the day before he disappeared, the dream, how he cheered me up, how lively he was. It felt a little better after taking with Sam.
