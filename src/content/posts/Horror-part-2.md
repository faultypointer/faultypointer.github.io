As I've already said in the previous  part, which was supposed to be about how I don't believe in ghosts and
stuff but instead became about how I would love for it to be real, I don't actually believe in the ghost or
any paranormal things. I'm not here to do the same thing again. Today, I'm here to do the opposite. I'm here 
to tell that although I am not a believer of such stuff, I'm not entirely indifferent to such things. In fact,
under just the right conditions, I find myself trying to not think of creepy things, like when I'm in bathroom
at night without any lights, or trying to rush the thing I'm currently doing to go somewhere "safe". Before I 
tell you why this is a good thing and why I'm happy that I'm still scared of such creepy things even though I 
don't believe in them, I gotta tell you first about ...

## My first and, as far as I remember, only nightmare
This was a very long time ago, I don't even remember how old I was.  To be completely honest, I don't even know
how it went. I don't remember what season it was but lets just say it was winter. So I wake up. I don't know how,
but I knew when mom came into the room I knew it was not really my mom. I think the thing posed as my mom chased
me or tried to harm me, as I've said I don't really remember anything. What I do remember was that I was very 
scared because of it. 

## Is it normal?
I don't know if it is normal to be sacred of things you don't really believe in. I'm not even that scared of 
the things that I can't go anywhere alone in dark. I could, I think, spent the night in a cemetery if I was sure
there were not any real dangers (snakes and insects and humans). But that does not mean I would not have been 
scared shitless the whole fucking time. But the thing is I would probably enjoy being scared. I think being scared
comes with a very good sleep. 

## The benefit
Being scared does come with huge benefits. No I'm not talking about the survival stuff and how fear is important.
I'm taking about how being scared of the things that aren't real is actually great. Some people aren't even phased
a little by the occult stuff and of them some can't enjoy a scary movie based around the paranormal stuff. I find
that to be really sad as there are such great movies like that. And even if people who aren't scared of such 
things can enjoy entertainment based around such things, as soon as the movie is over everything comes back to
reality. This is not true for me and for may others I think. If the movie was actually good and scary, even after
it ends the creepy feeling stays there for a long time. And for that I'm very grateful for the way I am.
